## Curated Transcription

### Page 1

- `Het maakt me niet uit.` = "I don't mind"; "it doesn't matter to me."
- `binnen` = inside.
- `toch` = really / indeed / after all, depending on tone.
- `Je ziet er goed uit.` = You look good / well.
- `Doe maar.` = Go ahead; that's fine.
- `Doe maar niet.` = Better not; no thanks.
- `Dat is lang geleden.` = That was a long time ago; long time no see.
- `Ik ben goed bezig.` = I am doing well; I am making good progress.
- `Zij ziet er nog goed uit.` = She still looks good.
- `Kom binnen.` = Come in.
- `Geef je jas maar hier.` = Just give me your coat.
- In `Geef je jas maar hier`, `maar` is not "but"; it softens the command and makes it sound welcoming.

### Page 2

Personal and possessive pronouns:

| Subject / object | Possessive |
| --- | --- |
| `ik` | `mijn` |
| `je` / `jij` / `jou` | `je` / `jouw` |
| `u` | `uw` |
| `hij` | `zijn` |
| `zij` / `ze` | `haar` |
| `wij` / `we` | `ons` / `onze` |
| `jullie` | `jullie` |
| `zij` / `ze` | `hun` |

Correction and improvement:

- Use `jou` after a preposition: `Hoe gaat het met jou?`
- `je` is the neutral, unstressed informal form. `jij`, `jou`, and `jouw` add emphasis.
- Use `ons` with singular `het` nouns: `ons huis`, `ons kind`.
- Use `onze` with `de` nouns and all plurals: `onze vriendin`, `onze kinderen`.

Useful phrases:

- `Hoe gaat het?` = How are you?
- `Hoe gaat het met jou?` = How are you?
- `Het gaat goed.` = It is going well.
- `Hoe is het met jullie allemaal?` = How are all of you?
- `Tot gauw.` = See you soon.

### Page 3

- `het spelletje` = little game; casual game.
- `voorzichtig` = careful / carefully.
- `bij` = at, with, near, by, or in, depending on context.
- `Ik ben bij mijn vriend.` = I am at my friend's place / with my friend.
- `Hij woont bij het station.` = He lives near the station.
- `Bij slecht weer blijven we binnen.` = In bad weather, we stay inside.
- `bij elkaar` = together.
- `bijna` = almost.
- `Hoe is het met jou?` / `Hoe gaat het met jou?` = How are you?
- `Ik ben aan het trainen.` = I am training.

Improvement:

- `zijn + aan het + infinitive` describes an action happening right now: `Ik ben aan het trainen.`

### Page 4

- `krijgen` = to get / receive.
- `visite krijgen` = to receive visitors / have visitors.
- `lief` = sweet, kind, dear.
- `Doe het raam dicht.` = Close the window.
- `dicht` can mean closed; `dichtbij` means nearby / close by.
- `Sta op.` = Stand up / get up.
- `waar` = true. This is different from `waar?` = where?
- `gebiedende wijs` = imperative / command form.
- `bewegen` = to move.
- `roepen` = to call / shout.
- `Ik heb zin om voetbal te kijken.` = I feel like watching football.
- `Ik heb zin in koffie/thee/cappuccino.` = I feel like coffee/tea/a cappuccino.

Corrections and improvements:

- `roept` should be understood as "calls" or "shouts"; "screams" is usually `schreeuwt`.
- `jarig` means "having one's birthday": `Ik ben jarig.`
- `de verjaardag` is the birthday.
- `de jarige` is the birthday person.
- For going to a birthday event, use `naar`: `Ik ga naar je verjaardag.`

### Grammar Patterns
- **Dit/dat en deze/die** (`grammar_pattern-dit-dat-en-deze-die`): This/that with de- and het-words
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: dit/dat + het-word; deze/die + de-word
  - Explanation: Use 'dit' and 'dat' with singular het-nouns, and 'deze' and 'die' with de-nouns and plurals.
  - Tags: A1, demonstratives, articles
  - Example: dit huis, dat huis = this house, that house [ai_generated]
  - Example: deze straat, die straat = this street, that street [ai_generated]
- **Tot en tot en met** (`grammar_pattern-tot-en-tot-en-met`): Until versus up to and including
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: tot + endpoint; tot en met + included endpoint
  - Explanation: 'Tot' normally excludes the endpoint, while 'tot en met' explicitly includes it.
  - Tags: A1, time
  - Example: Open tot vrijdag. = Open until Friday. [ai_generated]
  - Example: Open tot en met vrijdag. = Open through Friday, including Friday. [ai_generated]
- **Plaats aangeven** (`grammar_pattern-plaats-aangeven`): Expressing location
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: preposition + noun phrase
  - Explanation: Common location expressions include in, voor, achter, boven, naast, onder, tussen, tegen, aan, links van, rechts van, dicht bij, and ver van.
  - Tags: A1, location, prepositions
  - Example: De tas staat naast de doos. = The bag is next to the box. [ai_generated]
  - Example: De fiets staat tegen de muur. = The bicycle is leaning against the wall. [ai_generated]
- **Klokkijken met half, kwart, voor en over** (`grammar_pattern-klokkijken-met-half-kwart-voor-en-over`): Telling time with half, quarter, to, and past
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: [minutes] over/voor [hour]; half + next hour
  - Explanation: Dutch 'half zeven' means half an hour before seven, or 6:30. Use 'over' for minutes past and 'voor' for minutes to.
  - Tags: A1, time
  - Example: Het is half zeven. = It is half past six. [ai_generated]
  - Example: Het is kwart over negen. = It is quarter past nine. [ai_generated]
  - Example: Het is kwart voor vier. = It is quarter to four. [ai_generated]
- **Eruitzien als scheidbaar werkwoord** (`grammar_pattern-eruitzien-als-scheidbaar-werkwoord`): Using the separable verb eruitzien
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: subject + ziet/zien + er + complement + uit
  - Explanation: In a main clause, 'eruitzien' separates around the complement.
  - Tags: A1, separable_verbs, word_order
  - Example: Je ziet er goed uit. = You look well. [ai_generated]
  - Example: Ze ziet er nog moe uit. = She still looks tired. [ai_generated]

### Grammar Patterns
- **Meervoud met -en** (`grammar_pattern-meervoud-met-en`): Plural with -en
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: noun stem + en
  - Explanation: Many nouns form the plural with -en. Spelling may change to preserve the vowel sound.
  - Constraints: A doubled vowel in a closed syllable is often written as one vowel in an open plural syllable: boom - bomen.; A consonant may double after a short vowel: bos - bossen.
  - Tags: A1, plurals
  - Example: de plant - de planten = the plant - the plants [ai_generated]
  - Example: de boom - de bomen = the tree - the trees [ai_generated]
- **Meervoud met -s** (`grammar_pattern-meervoud-met-s`): Plural with -s
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: noun + s or 's
  - Explanation: Many nouns ending in an unstressed vowel or sounds such as -el, -en, -er, -je take -s. An apostrophe preserves a long vowel sound after a, i, o, u, or y.
  - Tags: A1, plurals
  - Example: de hobby - de hobby's = the hobby - the hobbies [ai_generated]
  - Example: de tafel - de tafels = the table - the tables [ai_generated]
- **Modaal werkwoord met infinitief** (`grammar_pattern-modaal-werkwoord-met-infinitief`): Modal verb with an infinitive
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: subject + conjugated modal + rest + infinitive
  - Explanation: The modal verb is conjugated and the second verb remains an infinitive at the end of the clause.
  - Tags: A1, modal_verbs, word_order
  - Example: Ik kan goed tekenen. = I can draw well. [ai_generated]
  - Example: Wij moeten deze week huiswerk maken. = We must do homework this week. [ai_generated]
- **Welk en welke** (`grammar_pattern-welk-en-welke`): Welk and welke
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: welk + het-word; welke + de-word/plural
  - Explanation: Use 'welk' with singular het-nouns and 'welke' with singular de-nouns and all plurals.
  - Tags: A1, question_words, articles
  - Example: Welk huis? = Which house? [ai_generated]
  - Example: Welke straat? = Which street? [ai_generated]
  - Example: Welke winkels? = Which shops? [ai_generated]

### Grammar Patterns
- **Persoonsvorm op de tweede plaats** (`grammar_pattern-persoonsvorm-op-de-tweede-plaats`): The finite verb is in second position
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: [position 1] + finite verb + subject/rest
  - Explanation: In a Dutch main clause, the conjugated verb normally occupies the second grammatical position.
  - Tags: A1, word_order
  - Example: Ik kook pasta. = I cook pasta. [ai_generated]
  - Example: In het weekend kook ik pasta. = At the weekend I cook pasta. [ai_generated]
- **Regelmatige werkwoorden in de tegenwoordige tijd** (`grammar_pattern-regelmatige-werkwoorden-in-de-tegenwoordige-tijd`): Regular verbs in the present tense
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: ik: stem; jij/hij/zij: stem + t; wij/jullie/zij: infinitive
  - Explanation: Use the stem with 'ik', add -t for most singular subjects, and use the infinitive for plural subjects.
  - Constraints: With inversion after jij/je, the -t is usually dropped: Werk jij?
  - Tags: A1, present_tense
  - Example: Ik werk. Zij werkt. Wij werken. = I work. She works. We work. [ai_generated]
- **Vragen met inversie** (`grammar_pattern-vragen-met-inversie`): Questions using inversion
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: question word + finite verb + subject + rest OR finite verb + subject + rest
  - Explanation: In a direct question, the finite verb comes before the subject.
  - Tags: A1, questions
  - Example: Waar werk je? = Where do you work? [ai_generated]
  - Example: Kun je morgen komen? = Can you come tomorrow? [ai_generated]
- **Meervoud gebruikt altijd 'de'** (`grammar_pattern-meervoud-gebruikt-altijd-de`): Plural nouns always use 'de'
  - Category: grammar
  - Pattern: de + plural noun
  - Explanation: All plural nouns take the definite article 'de', even when the singular uses 'het'.
  - Tags: A1, articles, plurals
  - Example: het kind - de kinderen = the child - the children [ai_generated]
